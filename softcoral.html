SOFT CORAL

Gorgonian ( Sea Fans)

  • This family of soft coral is also called sea whips or sea fans.
  • Individual tiny polyps form colonies that are normally erect, flattened, branching, and reminiscent of a fan.
  • Gorgonians are found primarily in shallow waters, though some have been found at depths of several thousand feet.
  • The more fan-shaped and flexible gorgonians tend to populate shallower areas with strong currents, while the taller, thinner, and stiffer gorgonians can be found in deeper, calmer waters.

Carnation Coral

  • Easily one of the most beautiful soft corals in the ocean the carnation coral comes in a spectacular range of colors and flourish below underhangs and caves.
  • They are found mostly in Indo-Pacific – Islands of the Indopacific including Fiji, Tonga, Solomon Islands and the Great Barrier Reef.
  • Unfortunately this specimen of soft coral is extremely sensitive to changes in water chemistry and is on the decline.

Tree Corals

  • They are usually attached to hard surfaces including boulders, jetty pilings and coral rubble.
  • A thick ‘main trunk’ attaches to a hard surface on one end, with many small branches on the other end which is why they are referred to as Tree Coral.
  • The Carnation Coral Dendronephthya belong to this family of soft corals.
